<p><p>Human embryonic stem cells (HESC) are derived from early-stage embryos and have the potential to develop into various cell types. The market is categorized into three main types: totipotent stem cells, which can form all cell types including placental tissue; pluripotent stem cells, capable of differentiating into nearly any cell type in the body; and unipotent stem cells, which can develop into only one cell type. Each type has unique applications in research, regenerative medicine, and therapeutic development.</p></p>
